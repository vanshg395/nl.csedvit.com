Startup Glossary

- Janhvi Mehta

ROI (Return On Investment)

What the investor can expect to get for what they put in. It can also be used to describe the results of a particular marketing campaign’s success. You want things to be “ROI positive.”

KPI(Key Performance Indicator)

This is a quantified value that indicates how effectively your company/product is achieving its benchmarks.

FinTech

FinTech is short for financial technology It's basically used to describe a new innovative technology in the financial space. It could be retail banking, a contemporary investment app, and most importantly Bitcoin.

Agile

Similar to the literal meaning, it is a philosophy of software development that showcases incremental development and emphasises adaptability and collaboration.

Dead Pool

Where companies that die go.

Drip Feed

When investors fund a startup a little bit at a time instead of in a lump sum.

Startups to Watchout For!

- Sahil Sinha

Nowadays, everybody dreams of launching a startup of their own but there are very few that catch the eye of the public. In fact, 30% of startups go under within the first two years of operation! However, every now and then, there come along a bunch of innovators that end up changing the world. Here are a few startups that look to do just that!


WiFi Dabba

Jio changed the rules of the internet industry by providing data packs at incredibly cheap prices, but this startup goes a step further and offers even better deals!

Their data packages include 100 MB for Rs 2; 500 MB for Rs 10; and 1 GB for Rs 20 - with a uniform validity of 24 hours.

In comparison, Jio provides 150 MB at Rs 19 to its pre-paid customers and 1.05 GB at Rs 52.


Their main aim is to provide super cheap and fast data, even to those that have just a couple bucks to spare! They keep their promise of fast data by using fiber optic cables, as they are more reliable than airwaves. The company has already installed 350 dabbas (router) across Bengaluru and they claim that they have over 1800 connection requests in-waiting!


Gayam Motor Works (GMW)

Gayam Motor Works is not focused on making a sedan or a sports car, but it has achieved something nobody could have ever thought of! They are the first company in the world to develop an electric three-wheeler with Li-Ion battery, with a battery swapping system.

In a research conducted by the Petroleum Conservation Research, it was found out that transportation contributes to 51% of pollution in India and, 75-80% in urban areas. Also, there are over 600,000 electric rickshaws which use lead-acid batteries and it takes around 8-9 hours for each vehicle to get fully charged. The batteries must be replaced twice a year.

What makes this company special is the fact that they have mastered battery swapping technology – a process where you swap a drained battery with a fully charged one. This may sound simple, but it is a very complex process. Even Tesla has only tested this and not made it commercial.


Algosurg Products Pvt Ltd

This startup helps in making knee surgery 20 times cheaper! They are on their way to find a replacement for the very expensive CT scans conducted during knee replacement surgeries. Their idea is to convert a 2D X-ray image to a 3D model for a bone.

This technology, referred to as ‘XrayTo3D’ is used in conjugation with a Cloud-based software called ‘Tabplan3D’, to generate a 3D model of bone on a mobile phone using just two X-ray images! This model can be viewed from any angle and can also be used for virtual simulation of the surgery.

HOW MARKETERS TARGET YOUR WALLET

- Janhvi Mehta

Ever wondered why you just can’t stop yourself from buying that 50 percent off imported juice that you don’t even like. Why do you think that it is so hard to stick to your shopping list?

Here are some tactics experts use to get money from our pockets.

How to get people to shop impulsively?

Customer psychologists have researched that over 50 percent of the purchases made are unplanned ones. Sometimes the aesthetic of the item and the glossy packaging will attract you towards it and voila, your cart is now overflowing with items that are of no use to you. Subconsciously, the architecture of the store can impact consumer satisfaction which in turn translates to impulsive shopping.

The Gruen Effect

In the twentieth century, the architect Victor Gruen used light and space to dramatically display the items on the display window. He did so to try to grab the attention of the bystanders and researched if that would convert them into customers. This phenomenon refers to the habits of a shopper who goes into the store with the intention of buying a specific item and comes out with multiple items.

“Almost 20% of buying needs are based on logic and needs and the other 80% of shopping decisions are based on emotions.” The creative director for Ikea, Richard La Graauw. So, most retailers try and tap into that weakness.

The jobs of such creative directors and marketers are actually very important as they decide how the product is going to be placed in the store. Apart from that, they analyse data using technology. Most stores have a huge database at their fingertips. They look at the path the customers take, the area and items that interest them.

Scent Marketing

This refers to the subconscious marketing that targets your nose to invoke an emotion. Researchers found that combining scent with visual marketing strengthens your memory of the product in the long term. We associate scents with emotions, for example, the scent of lemons, oranges or anything citrus could help your messy store appear clean. Customers in scented environments linger longer, perceive the product as better and are willing to pay higher prices.

Personalising the product

This refers to the customisation of the product in order to appeal to more customers. Case in point, Netflix uses artificial intelligence to customise every thumbnail for shows. So, each user has a different thumbnail that caters to their field of interest. For example, if you watch a lot of comedy stand ups chances are that for the thumbnail of a serious movie, you will get a thumbnail of a comic. This intrigues the user as the it makes their experience more tailored to their needs.

INSPIRING INDUSTRY INTERVEIWS

Aathif Ul Haq is the Managing Director of Grid Property Developers. He mirrors the values of his organisation and heads professionalism, entrepreneurship and passionate commitment in advancing the interests of all his clients.

Having completed his B. Com from Loyola College, Chennai, Aathif developed an interest in business which made him pursue Masters in International Business Management from the renowned Sheffield Hallam University, United Kingdom.

Coming from a high-profile business background held together from the past 80 years, his expertise in business management has made him a successful member in Managing Board of Directors of Hundreds Heritage, Hundreds Tea, Hundreds Fitness and Red Shoe.

He is also the Managing Director at C9 Bistro and Hundreds Fitness.

Following is an excerpt of his interview with CSED VIT*.

Hi Aathif! Where are you from?
Hi! I am from Vellore.

What are the advantages and disadvantages of sustaining a business in Vellore?
We have started businesses that previously did not exist in Vellore. For example, Heritage. Getting it to Vellore was expensive but it was something Vellore had never seen before. We incorporated different interiors and expensive stuff which gave us first mover advantage. The risk factor was there but we were welcomed gracefully. Same thing with fitness. There were gyms in Vellore but no fitness culture. Before heritage there was nothing on this road. Now, it’s completely different.

What were your ambitions when you were growing up?
I am someone who never wanted my father’s money even though I enjoyed it. I always wanted to make my father proud however it may be.

 Have you seen a growth in customers since you started?
Yes, of course. We have had our ups and downs with quality but made corrective actions according to the numbers of customers we cater to.

How did you get the idea of combining a café with a gym? What makes it work?
We have always been into hospitality, and footfall is very important for every business. If we just opened a café the customer would visit once or twice a week but that is not the case with the gym. We needed a USP so it was win-win situation in our eyes.

 How do you manage to sustain a business in Vellore with so much competition?
There is no competition. There are places like GRT and Fortune but we have our own customers. I believe their quality is no-where near compared to ours.

 How does the social environment of Vellore impact your business?
We try to balance the student and the family crowd. We wanted both crowds. Our quality and menu take care of both facets. We regularly consult technical people to make our quality better and improve.

What’s best advice you’ve gotten?
When I was leaving for university my father told me “the money and property might not be around when you are back. The only asset I can give you is education. Also, live your life to the fullest”.

How do you satisfy your customers?
By treating them like guests, not like customers.

 What’s the mentality that drives you to want to succeed?
I am very confident in whatever I do. I consider myself to be clear headed and act as a motivational mirror to everybody by giving opportunities to everyone.

Learning from failure is the key to success. What motivates you to keep working despite the hurdles in the way?
My instinct and passion.

*Answers as given to Janhvi Mehta

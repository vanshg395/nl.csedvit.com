Startups to Watchout For

- Priyesha Jethi

Like the start-up “Made in heaven”, in the new Amazon Prime’s series, here are the real-life start-ups to watch out for:


StuCred

Isn’t the main problem for every college student, their allowance? StuCred is a unique start-up that lends students with extra money without any interest. This provides them with financial flexibility and with a loan repay time of 30 days and credit limit boost system, you might wonder how they profit from it? Just like most, their source is advertisements too.

Dunzo

How would you like to get someone to run errands for you? Seems a bit of a stretch? With Dunzo, major cities in India like Bengaluru, Pune, Delhi, Gurgaon, Chennai and Hyderabad have such delivery services. Everyone’s waiting for it to be a nationwide trend. funded by Google, this start-up is making India lazy (but we’re loving it).

Décor-Matters

Everyone has a picture of their dream house or apartment in their minds. But we’re never really sure if it actually is going to look as perfect as in our heads. This is an app that provides users with a platform to experience their home decorations on their smartphones before actually buying the products. This is a great way to get an overview of your home and avoid the crazy colour contrast blunders!

Adhara

How stress-free would it be to roam around with no tension of getting your wallet stolen?
Adhara is one of the best start-ups and really works on the need of the hour by aiming to replace regular currency transactions with cryptocurrencies. Not-so-surprisingly, it has got huge funding from ConsenSys, that has a history of investing in crypto-based companies.

Zume Pizza

The fear of being taken over by robots is coming true with AI companies like Zume Pizza who are working on smart production and delivery of Pizza. They use automated ovens and self-cleaning robot cutters for this purpose. Just as much as we’ll enjoy the fact that our pizzas are being delivered by a robot, it is a big hit on the employment status of many people. And, don’t forget about the sudden hike in pizza prices!

Sports Startups

- Sahil Sinha

In India, people were obsessed with cricket to such an extent that the rest of the sports industry often got neglected. But the sporting scenario in our country is no doubt changing.

The recent medal-winning spree and some fantastic performances by our sportspersons in badminton, archery, weightlifting, tennis etc have given a boost to aspiring athletes and sports centric start-ups looking to make it big.

Here, we’re going to discuss some of the start-ups and how they are changing the sporting ecosystem of the country:

Sports365

Founded by Chandra Sekhar Reddy and Aashutosh Chaudari in 2011, Sports365 is all set to transform the face of Indian sports. Sports365 is India’s first professional company that caters to the sports and fitness sector by being a one stop solution for sporting requirements of both individuals and institutions.

They not only sell a wide range of sports products like sport and fitness equipment, apparels and accessories of leading national and international brands, but they also provide services for events, infrastructure development, and consulting and merchandising services to schools and colleges, corporates, clubs and real estates.

Xtreme Zone

It was founded by Praveen Krishnaiah and Deepa Praveen. This start-up has been a pioneer in adventure sports and is the largest Indian company in this field. They offer paintball, wall climbing, bungee jumping from towers and cranes across India, artificial wall climbing etc.

The company provides thrilling sports experiences for the youth by making safe, affordable and quality services accessible to them. They also offer exciting outdoor experiences for corporates as well as outdoor enthusiasts.

Protégé Sports

It was founded by Pranesh Krishnamurthy, Rohit Narayan, and Rahul Rane. It was launched with the vision to bring sports technology to all. They supply wearable technology for team sports like hockey and football which helps to track the players’ performance on the field. This, in turn, enables clubs and coaches to set a benchmark for players and manage trial sessions and training accordingly.

It also provides SAAS (software as a service) tools such as software licenses to use its academy/club management tools, communication modules and training modules. It also offers tournament management services, along with merchandising and ticketing services.

Fantain Sports

It was founded by Anand Ramachandran, Vivek Venugopalan and Aaravind Ramachandra in 2013. Fantain Sports is a fan relationship management and analytics start-up. Sports companies use ‘Fan Data’ to increase predictability and revenue by using its ‘Fan Data Management Platform’. It collects and analyses ‘Fan Data’ from various sources and provides actionable insights to customers. In 2016, Fantain Sports was acquired by BookMyShow.

There are a lot of start-ups emerging in this field and people have now started opening their start-ups in this industry rather than opening a start-up in the mainstream industries.

ON THE PECK?

- Rashi

“Exhausting”.

The only reply most of us get for “How was your week?”

Stress can affect people on one level, not only mentally, or on multiple levels such as both physically and emotionally. Most people find various coping mechanisms like smoking, illicit drug usage, excessive alcohol etc. which might prove to be very unhealthy in the long run.

These issues, if ignored, might lead to serious secondary mental and physical issues like anxiety, depression, insomnia, fatigue, headaches and others.

The response is often immediate and uncontrollable. Consistently, high levels of stress can cause people to develop serious conditions such as hypertension, stroke, diabetes, chronic pain, and even suffer from heart attacks.

A good therapy can help people identify the wrong coping mechanisms and instead develop a healthy way to manage stress.

Now, coming to all the small triggers which lead to that “everyday” tension- Did all the bad days at work, quarrels, arguments and the pressure lead to you slamming your phone at the wall?

Then you might want these exact things on your desktop to save you a really bad guilt trip and a good fortune, maybe.


Stress Balls

Squeezing releases tension. Now some people might think selling these smiley faced balls is just a gimmick to pull out extra cash from overly exhausted workers, but they work. Like magic.

Desktop Punching Ball

This is going to save a lot of your favorite things from landing on that cold hard floor, shattered. And hopeless. Save the punches!

Fidget Spinners and Cubes

Fidgeting increases attentiveness. It creates a good distraction for the ‘Bored’ part of your brain. The fidget cubes in fact have six different activities to keep your brain engaged.

It’s Slime Time

Sticky and messy. Yes. Unless you’ve got those slime hands. Watching all the adults exhaust themselves every single day, growing up can be hard too. Your children are watching you. Inculcate mindfulness. Let them grow. Tug, pull, squish and bring the calm!

Tangle Therapy

Strangle. Crumple. Squeeze that frustration out. You got it.

Magnetic Desk Sculptures

Encourages critical thinking and creativity. Build something out of those tiny little magnetic balls. Think out of the box. Grab all that negativity and turn into art!.

Scalp Massager

Now who wouldn’t love a nice massage after a really exhausting mind work? The tingling sensations induce a relaxing feeling. Also, even dogs love it!

Chinese Meditation Balls

These have been around for quite a while now. You just rub them between your fingers and the soothing sound does all the work!
